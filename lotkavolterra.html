Chaos in Lotka-Volterra Food Webs

The standard Lotka-Volterra model is a canonical example of a two-dimensional system of ordinary differential equations. \begin{align*} {\dot x}_1 = \alpha x_1 - \beta x_1 x_2\\ {\dot x}_2 = -\gamma x_2 + \mu x_1 x_2 \end{align*} \(x_1\) is the prey population and \(x_2\) the predator population. However, ecosystems are complex. Predators can exist along the hierarchy, as another animals own prey. We can increase the dimensionality of the system by introducing a third species. This species can take on many different roles in the ecosystem: predation, parasitism, symbiosis. We wish to examine the case of a super-predator, an animal which all other animals in the ecosystem. To model this behavior, we can modify the Lotka-Volterra model. \begin{align*} {\dot x}_1 &= x_1 - \alpha_1 x_1 - \alpha_2 x_1 x_2 - \alpha_3 x_1 x_3 \\ {\dot x}_2 &= -\alpha_4 x_2 + \alpha_5 x_1 x_2 - \alpha_6 x_2 x_3 \\ {\dot x}_3 &= -\alpha_7 x_3 + \alpha_8 x_2 x_3 + \alpha_9 x_1 x_3 \\ \end{align*} To study this solution, we can generate different simulations for a variety of parameter values. We than can study their behavior through Poincare Maps. First, let us non-dimensionalize the equation. \begin{align*} {\dot x}_1 &= x_1( 1 - x_1 - x_2 - x_3) \\ {\dot x}_2 &= x_2(-{\tilde \alpha_1} + {\tilde \alpha_2} x_1 - {\tilde \alpha_3}x_3) \\ {\dot x}_3 &= x_3(-{\tilde \alpha_4} + {\tilde \alpha_5}x_2 + {\tilde \alpha_6}x_1 ) \\ \end{align*} Now, we can begin bifurcation analysis. Let the vector of parameters \({\bf \tilde \alpha} = [5, {\tilde \alpha_2}, 4, 3, 4, 20]\). We can then slowly increase the parameter \({\tilde \alpha_2}\), the predation of the predator upon the prey, and see how it changes the overall trajectory of the system.
